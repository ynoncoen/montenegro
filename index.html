Montenegro Trip Itinerary

Welcome to your 6-day Montenegro adventure! This itinerary is designed for 2 adults and 4 teenagers (ages 12-16), balancing exploration of main sites, beach relaxation in Budva, and thrilling activities like rafting and ziplining in the north. Enjoy your trip from May 29 to June 4, 2025!

Flight Information: You will be flying with Arkia Airlines. Please note that all times are local to their respective airports, and there is a 1-hour time zone difference between Israel (ahead) and Montenegro.

Day 1: Arrival and Budva Exploration (May 29, 2025)

  • Morning: Depart from TLV (Tel Aviv) at 08:55 on flight IZ369 (Arkia). Land at TIV (Tivat) at 06:45 (local time). Pick up your rental car and drive to Budva (30 minutes).
  • Afternoon: Check into your accommodation in Budva (e.g., a hotel or apartment for 6). Explore Budva’s Old Town—wander its narrow streets, visit the Citadel, and enjoy the coastal vibe.
  • Evening: Relax on a nearby beach like Mogren or Slovenska Plaza, followed by dinner at a local restaurant (try grilled seafood or ćevapi).
  • Stay: Budva.

Note: The arrival time in Tivat (06:45) appears earlier than the departure time from Tel Aviv (08:55) due to Montenegro being 1 hour behind Israel.

Day 2: Kotor and Perast Day Trip (May 30, 2025)

  • Morning: Drive to Kotor (30 minutes). Explore the UNESCO-listed Old Town, including St. Tryphon’s Cathedral. If the teens are up for it, hike to the fortress (1-2 hours round trip) for panoramic views.
  • Afternoon: Drive to Perast (15 minutes from Kotor). Take a short boat ride to Our Lady of the Rocks, a picturesque island church, and have lunch in Perast or Kotor.
  • Evening: Return to Budva for dinner and a relaxed evening.
  • Stay: Budva.

Day 3: Beach Day in Budva (May 31, 2025)

  • Morning: Enjoy a leisurely morning at Mogren Beach (scenic and quieter) or Slovenska Plaza (lively and family-friendly).
  • Afternoon: Optionally, drive to Sveti Stefan (15 minutes) for photos of this iconic island village, or stay at the beach.
  • Evening: Dinner at a beachfront restaurant in Budva with sunset views.
  • Stay: Budva.

Day 4: Ziplining at Lake Piva and Rafting Camp (June 1, 2025)

  • Morning: Check out from Budva and drive to Plužine (2.5 hours). Experience an exciting zipline over Lake Piva (around 1,400m long with stunning views).
  • Afternoon: Drive to Brštanovica Rafting Camp near the Tara River (1.5 hours from Plužine). Check in and settle into the camp.
  • Evening: Enjoy dinner at the camp, surrounded by nature.
  • Stay: Brštanovica Rafting Camp.

Day 5: Rafting on the Tara River and Return to Tivat (June 2, 2025)

  • Morning: After breakfast, go rafting on the Tara River. This half-day trip (3-4 hours) covers 18 km of rapids in Europe’s deepest canyon—perfect for ages 12+.
  • Afternoon: After lunch (usually included), drive to Tivat (3.5 hours). Check into your accommodation.
  • Evening: Dinner in Tivat, perhaps at Porto Montenegro’s waterfront.
  • Stay: Tivat.

Day 6: Tivat Exploration and Departure Prep (June 3, 2025)

  • Morning: Explore Tivat at a relaxed pace. Visit Porto Montenegro, a modern marina with shops and cafes, or unwind at a nearby beach.
  • Afternoon: Optionally, drive to Herceg Novi (40 minutes) for a quick stroll through its Old Town, or stay in Tivat to rest.
  • Evening: Have a farewell dinner in Tivat at a waterfront restaurant.
  • Stay: Tivat.

Day 7: Departure (June 4, 2025)

  • Early Afternoon: Drive to Tivat airport (10-15 minutes), arriving by 12:50 PM for your 14:50 flight. Return the rental car and depart on flight IZ368 (Arkia) from TIV (Tivat) to TLV (Tel Aviv), arriving at 11:00 (local time).

Note: The departure time from Tivat (14:50) and arrival time in Tel Aviv (11:00) reflect a 1-hour time zone difference, with Israel being ahead of Montenegro.

Practical Tips

  • Transportation: A rental car from Tivat airport is ideal for this itinerary, especially for the northern leg. Public buses are less practical here.
  • Bookings: Reserve rafting and ziplining ahead of time—late May/early June is the start of peak season.
  • Weather: Bring swimwear and sunscreen for the coast, plus jackets for the cooler northern mountains.
  • Costs: Plan for car rental, accommodations, activities (roughly €75-100 per person for rafting and ziplining), and meals.
  • Food: Sample Montenegrin cuisine—burek, fresh fish, and local wines are must-tries.
